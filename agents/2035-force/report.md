# 2035 Force-Structure Content Review, Organization, and Synchronization Report

## Executive Summary

The current 2035 force-structure material has grown into a strong near-future reference package covering:

- Mechanized platoon
- Infantry squad
- Vehicle roster
- Platoon tactics
- Squad battle drills
- Mechanized platoon battle drills
- Combined-arms integration
- Recon platoon
- Weapons platoon
- Cavalry platoon

The overall concept is coherent and compelling: a **networked, drone-rich, EW-aware, manned-unmanned force** built for a high-lethality battlespace.

The main task now is **not expansion, but synchronization**.

This content should be treated as a developing canon set that needs:
1. consistent naming,
2. defined echelon roles,
3. reduced overlap,
4. clearer realism boundaries,
5. cleaner file organization.

- Consistent tactical tone

## Key Synchronization Findings

## 1. Platform Naming Needs Standardization

Several overlapping terms are used for related or identical systems:
- XM30
- OMFV
- NGIFV
- XM30-Scout
- XM30-AT
- OMFV-C2

This is manageable, but only if one naming convention is treated as canon.

### Recommended naming standard
Use **XM30** as the base platform family name.

Suggested format:
- **XM30 IFV** — standard mechanized infantry platform
- **XM30 C2** — command-and-control variant
- **XM30 Scout** — reconnaissance/scout variant
- **XM30 AT** — anti-armor/fire support variant

### Usage guidance
- Treat **OMFV** as historical/procurement context, not the primary operational name
- Remove or reduce use of **NGIFV** unless it is intentionally used as a broad category term
- Keep variant naming consistent across all files

---

## 2. Recon and Cavalry Should Be Separated by Echelon and Role

The earlier overlap between Recon Platoon and Cavalry Platoon is best resolved by **separating them by echelon and operational purpose**, not merging them.

### Canon decision
- **Recon Platoon** = lighter, stealthier, information-first formation
- **Cavalry Platoon** = more heavily armed security/screening formation tied to armored/mechanized maneuver

### Recommended distinction sentence
> **Recon Platoon** exists to build and preserve the picture.  
> **Cavalry Platoon** exists to fight for the picture when necessary and hand the fight to mechanized forces on favorable terms.

### Recon Platoon emphasis
- low-signature movement
- route and zone reconnaissance
- observation posts
- emitter mapping
- persistent ISR
- stealth and displacement over direct fighting
- lighter or lower-signature vehicle package

### Cavalry Platoon emphasis
- screening and guard missions
- aggressive reconnaissance
- shaping contact for mechanized formations
- stronger self-protection and firepower
- direct support to XM30/MPF maneuver elements
- limited fighting for information before displacement

### Recommended differentiation table

| Formation | Primary role | Signature | Armor / firepower | Contact preference | Typical distance forward |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|---:|
| Recon Platoon | Find, track, report | Lowest possible | Light-moderate | Avoid, observe, displace | 2–5 km+ |
| Cavalry Platoon | Screen, guard, secure | Low but less restrictive | Moderate-high | Fight briefly for information | 1–3 km+ |

This distinction should be reflected explicitly in both documents.

---

## 3. Echelon Ownership Needs Clarification

Several capabilities appear at multiple levels without clearly defined ownership:
- drones/UAS
- loitering munitions
- EW/counter-UAS
- robotic vehicles
- anti-armor systems

This makes the force feel broad, but not always tightly organized.

### Recommended ownership model

#### Squad
- micro-UAS
- handheld EW/counter-UAS tools
- lightweight anti-armor
- limited loitering munition access
- close-terrain clearance tools

#### Platoon
- shared ISR drone capacity
- robotic scout/gun systems
- platoon-level loitering munition tasking
- localized sensor fusion
- immediate anti-armor overwatch

#### Company / weapons platoon / attached support
- deeper strike drones
- heavier anti-armor density
- mortar-based precision fires
- stronger EW packages
- sustained counter-UAS coverage

This clarification would significantly improve realism and readability.

---

## 4. Structure and Tactics Should Stay Distinct

The current content naturally separates into:
- structure / composition,
- capabilities,
- battle drills,
- integration.

That separation should be preserved.

### Recommended rule
For each unit:
1. **Organization** — what it consists of
2. **Capabilities** — what it can do
3. **Tactics / battle drills** — how it fights
4. **Integration** — how it supports or is supported by other formations

This will keep the growing document set manageable.

---

## 5. Realism Boundary Should Be Defined Once

Some capabilities are plausible but still speculative for a 2035 force:
- widespread active protection systems
- frequent robotic wingmen at lower echelons
- routine AI-assisted targeting
- heavy counter-UAS integration across many platforms
- broad use of loitering munitions at lower tactical levels

These are acceptable for a near-future setting, but the assumption level should be made explicit.

### Recommendation
Add a short canon assumptions page defining the setting as one of the following:
- **Plausible 2035 projection**
- **Speculative but doctrinally grounded 2035 projection**
- **Alternate near-future Army force design**

The current material reads best as:
> **A speculative but doctrinally grounded 2035 U.S. Army-inspired force design.**

That phrasing preserves realism while allowing future-facing systems to remain central.

- more robust self-protection,

## Suggested Canon Principles Page

A short doctrine page could define the entire force in six lines:

1. **See first**
2. **Disperse by default**
3. **Robots take first risk**
4. **Strike with standoff before closing**
5. **Dismounts clear what vehicles cannot**
6. **Never remain where you were first detected**

This would give the whole force package a single, recognizable doctrinal spine.

---

## Final Recommendation

The material is strong and worth preserving. It already feels like the beginning of a coherent 2035 operational reference set. The next step should be **editorial consolidation**, not uncontrolled expansion.

### Priority actions
1. standardize the XM30-family naming system,
2. preserve Recon Platoon and Cavalry Platoon as separate formations with distinct roles,
3. define echelon ownership of UAS, EW, loitering munitions, and anti-armor assets,
4. keep structure, tactics, and battle drills in separate files,
5. create a short canon assumptions page and doctrinal principles page.

If these actions are completed, the result will be a clean, scalable, and internally consistent 2035 force-design framework.
