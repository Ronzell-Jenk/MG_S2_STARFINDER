# 2035 CAMD Higher Command and Tasking

## Purpose
Define the command echelons above the Combined Arms Maneuver Detachment (CAMD), how mission tasking flows to the detachment, and what support each echelon provides.

---

## Command Echelons Above CAMD

### Battalion (Primary Tasking Authority)
- **Commander:** Lieutenant Colonel (LTC)
- **Role:** Tactical control and routine tasking authority for most CAMD missions.
- **Provides:** Mission orders, PIRs, fires coordination, logistics, medical evacuation, communications support, and higher-tier UAS/EW feeds.
- **Key Staff:** S2, S3, Fires Cell, S4, S6, UAS Cell.

### Brigade Combat Team (Operational Control)
- **Commander:** Colonel (COL)
- **Role:** Operational-level control for missions beyond battalion scope.
- **Provides:** Operational ISR, long-range fires, cyber/EW effects, engineers, aviation support, and sustainment brigade support.
- **Typical Use Cases:** Deep reconnaissance, large-sector screening, shaping operations, counter-recon, urban isolation.

### Division (Theater-Level Tasking)
- **Commander:** Major General (MG)
- **Role:** Assigns major objectives, synchronizes brigades, integrates joint assets.
- **Provides:** Theater ISR, long-range precision fires, cyber/space/EW support, aviation brigade support, sustainment brigades, and operational logistics.
- **Typical Use Cases:** Independent CAMD operations, deep shaping, high-tempo exploitation in dispersed environments.

### Corps (Strategic Tasking & Support)
- **Commander:** Lieutenant General (LTG)
- **Role:** Campaign-level tasking and deep multi-domain support.
- **Provides:** Strategic fires, joint air support, theater ISR, large-scale EW, inter-service coordination, and theater sustainment.
- **Typical Use Cases:** Strategic reconnaissance, special-purpose raids, deep disruption missions, high-priority shaping operations.

---

## Tasking Flow
1. **Division** defines the operational problem.
2. **Brigade** assigns the mission framework.
3. **Battalion** issues the order.
4. **CAMD** executes with mission-command autonomy.
5. **Recon → Weapons → Mech → CAV** reporting and effects flow back upward.

## Support Relationships

### Battalion Support
- Fuel, ammunition, maintenance
- Medical evacuation
- Mortar and battalion fires
- UAS feeds
- EW support

### Brigade Support
- Engineers
- Aviation
- HIMARS / long-range fires
- Cyber/EW
- Sustainment brigade support

### Division Support
- Long-range precision fires
- Space/cyber effects
- Air support
- Operational logistics

### Corps Support
- Strategic fires
- Joint assets
- Theater sustainment

---

## Command Philosophy in Context
The CAMD is designed as a **semi-independent maneuver force** that interprets commander’s intent, self-synchronizes, and executes distributed operations while integrating fires, EW, robotics, and real-time reporting.
