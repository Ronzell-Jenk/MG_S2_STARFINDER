# 2035 CAMD Higher Command and Tasking

## Purpose
Describe how higher headquarters tasks the CAMD Task Force and how responsibilities are distributed during execution.

## Higher-Headquarters Intent
Higher command uses the task force to solve maneuver problems that require:
- persistent contact and reconnaissance,
- rapid combined-arms concentration at decisive terrain,
- secure transition between shaping, assault, and consolidation.

## Standard Tasking Inputs
Every mission order should define:
- **Task and purpose** (what to do and why),
- **Priority axis/objective** (where to focus main effort),
- **Time conditions** (latest line-of-departure and decision windows),
- **Control constraints** (boundaries, fire support coordination, no-strike limits),
- **Success and handoff criteria** (what must be true to transition).

## Command Relationships
- Task Force Commander controls all attached maneuver and support elements.
- Recon and Cavalry retain internal tactical autonomy while operating within task-force control measures.
- Fires/EW/UAS effects are centrally synchronized by the integration cell and released by commander authority.
- Sustainment node reports status to the XO and shifts support by phase and consumption rates.

## Task Distribution Model
- **Recon Platoon:** find, confirm, and track enemy dispositions and routes.
- **Cavalry Platoon:** screen, fix, and maintain security contact lines.
- **Mechanized Platoon:** execute main assault or counter-maneuver action.
- **Weapons Detachment:** anti-armor shaping, precision strike, and overwatch fires.
- **Support Node:** sustain momentum and prevent maneuver pause.

## Reporting and Decision Rhythm
- Contact report on first enemy signature and every major disposition change.
- Decision update at each phase-line crossing.
- Sustainment status report before assault and after objective seizure.
- Handoff report when transitioning to follow-on force or defensive posture.

## Command Principle
Tasking should assign outcomes, control measures, and limits, while allowing subordinate leaders freedom to adapt routes, formations, and timing to local contact.
