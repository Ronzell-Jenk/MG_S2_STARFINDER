# ⭐ 2035 Combined Arms Maneuver Detachment (CAMD)

A flexible, lethal, sensor-rich, manned–unmanned maneuver detachment at battalion-minus scale, integrating Mechanized Platoon, Recon Platoon, Weapons Platoon, and Cavalry Platoon under a Major with two Captains (XO & Infantry/Maneuver).

## 8. How the Detachment Fights

### Offense
1. **CAV screens** 5–10 km forward, reporting contact and route conditions.
2. **Recon defines the fight** — maps enemy dispositions, target signatures, and obstacle belts.
3. **Weapons shapes** with ATGM, smart mortars, and loitering munitions against anti-armor and control nodes.
4. **Mech executes** the decisive assault on the selected axis.
5. **C2 fuses** all data and directs kill chains throughout.

### Defense
1. **CAV detects** approaching enemy, triggers early warning.
2. **Recon maps and tracks** enemy reserves and approach routes.
3. **Weapons sets** anti-armor kill zones along primary avenues.
4. **Mech counterattacks** to restore position or blocks to preserve key terrain.

### Transition (Offense to Defense / Defense to Offense)
- Screen lines handed off from CAV to Recon or Mech as the situation dictates.
- Kill zones repositioned to cover new threat axis within one phase line.
- Sustainment node pre-positioned before transition to prevent maneuver pause.

## 9. Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)

### 9.1 Movement SOP
- **Formation:** Recon element leads at 5–10 km distance; CAV on flanks; Mech follows in column; Weapons in trail.
- **Spacing:** Minimum 200 m between vehicles in open terrain; tighten to 100 m in restricted terrain.
- **Comms:** SITREP every 30 minutes on movement net; immediate report on first contact.
- **Drone coverage:** At least one Group 2 UAS airborne over lead element at all times during movement.
- **Halts:** Recon and CAV establish 360° observation before main body halts; vehicles offset from road.

### 9.2 Actions on Contact SOP
1. Element in contact reports: direction, distance, size, activity, location, time, equipment (DDSAT-E).
2. CAV or Recon fixes the enemy; main body does not commit until signature confirmed.
3. Weapons element orients and prepares fires on likely anti-armor positions.
4. Commander approves engagement; designates main effort axis.
5. Mech moves to assault position under concealment; RCV-M leads into the breach.
6. Fires suppresses or destroys before assault crosses the line of departure.

### 9.3 Assault SOP
1. XM30 IFVs advance with RCV-M screening forward; RCV-H provides direct overwatch.
2. Dismounts clear buildings or treelines on order; UAS maintains persistent overwatch above.
3. Loitering munitions on standby for pop-up anti-armor threats.
4. Breach completed when Mech holds objective and Recon confirms no follow-on threat.
5. CAV repositions to new screen line 5–10 km beyond objective.

### 9.4 Defense SOP
1. CAV establishes screen and observation posts along likely enemy approaches.
2. Recon deploys sensors and dismount scouts to track enemy movement.
3. Weapons emplaces XM30-AT and RWS vehicles in hull-down or defilade positions on primary avenues.
4. Mech occupies battle positions with clear fields of fire; RCV-H used as forward sensor/decoy.
5. Commander designates trigger lines for fires and counterattack launch points.
6. Sustainment node positioned to the rear but reachable within 30 minutes.

## 10. Mission Profiles

### 10.1 Urban Operations
**Key Principles:**
- Drones enter first; XM30s follow; dismounts clear on order.
- RCV-M leads into blind corners and stairwells.
- EW cell disrupts enemy comms and UAS before assault.
- Precision strike (loitering munitions) replaces indirect fire in populated structures to limit collateral damage.

**Sequence:**
1. Recon maps building layout and enemy positions via UAS and SIGINT.
2. Weapons isolates the objective block; loitering munitions destroy crew-served weapons.
3. Mech breaches with RCV-M and XM30; dismounts clear room-to-room.
4. CAV secures outer cordon and prevents enemy withdrawal or reinforcement.

**Decision Points:**
- Commit dismounts only after RCV-M clears ground floor.
- Escalate to XM30 direct fire if resistance exceeds dismount capacity.

---

### 10.2 Forest / Restricted Terrain Operations
**Key Principles:**
- Vehicles maintain visual contact; separation reduced to 50–75 m.
- Dismounts lead with UAS confirming 300 m ahead.
- Loitering munitions used to suppress canopy-masked positions.
- CAV screens open flanks; Recon clears choke points before vehicle passage.

**Sequence:**
1. Recon clears dismount corridors and marks vehicle-passable routes.
2. Mech advances in column; UAS provides persistent top-cover.
3. Weapons holds at forest edge, covering approaches from open terrain.
4. Dismounts clear to depth; XM30s advance on confirmed-clear routes.

**Decision Points:**
- Halt vehicle advance if Recon cannot confirm route passability.
- Use RCV-H to test suspected IED or obstacle zones before vehicle entry.

---

### 10.3 Desert / Open Terrain Operations
**Key Principles:**
- Maximum standoff; XM30-AT engages at 4–6 km.
- Dispersed formation (400–600 m spacing) to reduce artillery vulnerability.
- UAS provides continuous 360° surveillance; CAV operates at maximum screen distance.
- Speed and dispersion are the primary protections.

**Sequence:**
1. CAV screens to maximum distance; Recon tracks enemy main body.
2. Weapons engages anti-armor assets and crew-served positions at long range.
3. Mech closes under fires; RCV-M absorbs first contact as XM30 IFVs flank.
4. Assault completes rapidly; Mech disperses to avoid artillery restrikes.

**Decision Points:**
- Disengage if enemy fires achieve suppression before Mech can close to 1 km.
- Transition to bounding overwatch if enemy positions are reinforced.

---

### 10.4 Night Operations
**Key Principles:**
- Full thermal/NVG integration across all elements.
- UAS with thermal imaging maintains persistent tracking of enemy positions.
- EW cell suppresses enemy night-vision and drone capabilities.
- Noise and light discipline strictly enforced from start to consolidation.

**Sequence:**
1. Recon inserts by darkness H−4; confirms objective is as planned.
2. Weapons pre-positions in overwatch before last light.
3. Mech moves to assault position H−1 under blackout.
4. Assault initiated with EW suppression and loitering munition suppression simultaneously.
5. CAV closes cordon at H-hour; consolidation complete before first light.

**Decision Points:**
- Abort and reset if Recon identifies objective deviation > 30% at H−1.
- Accelerate consolidation if enemy QRF is detected within 10 km.

## 11. Doctrinal Principles

1. **Find First. Strike First. Never Be Fixed.**
   Persistent ISR and EW dominance allows preemptive shaping before the enemy can set defenses.

2. **Robotics Absorb Risk; Humans Make Decisions.**
   RCV-M and RCV-H lead in high-threat environments; human crews authorize all lethal effects.

3. **Drones Provide the Picture; XM30s Provide the Punch.**
   UAS develops targets; the XM30 family delivers effects. Neither operates effectively without the other.

4. **Dispersed But Connected.**
   Elements operate across extended frontages but remain synchronized through shared COP and tight comms discipline.

5. **Information Dominance Beats Armor Thickness.**
   Sensor fusion, EW, and target-quality reporting create decision overmatch faster than any platform upgrade alone.

6. **Recon Pull, Not Attacker Push.**
   Mech does not commit until Recon confirms the target and the approach. Impetuous assault is a failure of doctrine.

## 12. Battle Drill Cards (Quick Reference)

### Card A — Movement to Contact
```
1. CAV: screen fwd (5–10 km), report contact.
2. RECON: confirm size/composition, designate target.
3. WEAPONS: orient, prepare fires.
4. COMD: approve engagement, assign axis.
5. MECH: occupy assault position, RCV-M fwd.
6. FIRES: suppress/destroy on COMD order.
7. MECH: assault. CAV: reposition screen.
```

### Card B — React to Ambush (Vehicle)
```
1. Vehicle in contact: return fire immediately, report direction.
2. All vehicles: bound to cover/concealment.
3. RCV-M: orient toward ambush, return fire.
4. WEAPONS: suppress ambush site with RWS / loitering munition.
5. COMD: direct break-contact or assault-through on order.
6. Medic: respond to casualties after immediate threat suppressed.
7. Recon/UAS: locate ambush support element.
```

### Card C — Breach and Assault
```
1. RECON: confirm objective, mark breach point.
2. WEAPONS: suppress anti-armor threats.
3. RCV-M: lead through breach.
4. XM30 IFV: follow, suppress building/treeline.
5. DISMOUNTS: clear on order, UAS overhead.
6. COMD: consolidate, re-establish screen, report.
```

### Card D — Break Contact
```
1. Rear element: provides suppression.
2. Lead element: bounds rearward by platoon.
3. UAS: tracks enemy pursuit.
4. WEAPONS: provides overwatch fires during withdrawal.
5. CAV: establishes new screen line.
6. COMD: confirms new consolidation point, issues follow-on order.
```
